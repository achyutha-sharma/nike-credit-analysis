# Nike-credit-analysis

A credit analysis evaluating Nike’s ability to repay a hypothetical $100 million commercial loan.

Project Overview

I reviewed Nike’s recent financial performance, credit ratios, and key business risks using data from the company’s 2025 Form 10-K.

The analysis focused on:

Revenue, EBIT, net income, cash, and debt trends

Liquidity, leverage, and profitability ratios

Consumer demand, competition, and wholesale-channel risks

Nike’s overall ability to repay the proposed loan

Key Findings

Nike’s 2025 results showed declines in revenue, EBIT, net income, and cash. However, the company maintained strong liquidity, significant cash reserves, and manageable debt levels.

The proposed $100 million loan was also small relative to Nike’s total assets and available cash.

Recommendation

Approve with conditions.

Recommended conditions:

Maximum Debt-to-EBITDA ratio of 2.5x
Quarterly financial reporting
Continued monitoring of liquidity and profitability
Lender rights if financial covenants are breached
